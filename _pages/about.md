I am a recent M.S. Computer Science graduate from California State University, Fullerton, 
specializing in AI/ML, natural language processing, and sustainable AI research.

My research investigates the carbon footprint of large language models. My co-authored paper, 
**"Greener AI: Evaluating Carbon Emissions and Energy Efficiency in Large Language Models"**, 
was accepted at IEEE ICECET 2026. A key finding: INT8/INT4 quantization — commonly assumed 
to reduce energy use — paradoxically increases carbon emissions on Tesla T4 GPUs due to 
dequantization overhead, with FP16 emerging as the practical efficiency sweet spot.

On the engineering side, I built a production-style RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) 
pipeline from scratch using PyMuPDF, sentence-transformers, ChromaDB, and Azure OpenAI 
GPT-4o-mini — deliberately without LangChain to maintain full architectural transparency 
and explainability.

I hold a Microsoft Azure AI-900 certification and have hands-on experience with PyTorch, 
Hugging Face Transformers, Python, and SQL. Previously, I served as a Teaching Associate 
at CSU Fullerton, instructing an introductory C++ course.

I am actively seeking roles in AI/ML engineering, NLP, or software engineering where I 
can contribute to meaningful, real-world systems.
